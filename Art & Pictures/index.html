Mona Lisa

Leonardo da Vinci's "Mona Lisa" is one of the most iconic paintings in the world, known for its enigmatic smile and captivating gaze. Painted between 1503 and 1506, this masterpiece depicts Lisa Gherardini, the wife of Florentine merchant Francesco del Giocondo.

The Kiss

Gustav Klimt's "The Kiss" is a stunning example of the artist's decorative and symbolic style, created during his "Golden Phase" from 1907 to 1908. The painting depicts a couple locked in a tender embrace, their bodies intertwined and enveloped in a golden cloak decorated with intricate patterns and symbols.

The Scream

Edvard Munch's "The Scream" is a haunting and iconic masterpiece that expresses the artist's profound sense of anxiety and existential dread. Painted in 1893, the painting depicts a figure standing on a bridge, their face contorted in a silent scream against a blood-red sky.

Guernica

Pablo Picasso's "Guernica" is a powerful and poignant anti-war painting that depicts the horrors of the bombing of the town of Guernica during the Spanish Civil War. Painted in 1937, the painting is a stark black, white, and grey composition that features distorted and anguished figures, animals, and buildings.
